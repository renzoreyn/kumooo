# Writing style guide

These rules are mandatory for Kumooo copy (docs, UI, marketing, errors).

Write like a technical founder. Not a marketing department. Not an AI.

## Tone

Confident, relaxed, direct, technically competent.
Slightly sarcastic. Self-aware. Occasionally funny.
Never cringe. Never arrogant.

Humor is seasoning. Not the meal.

## Audience

Developers, freelancers, agencies, technical founders, power users.
Assume they're smart. Never talk down.

## Rules

- Short sentences. Use contractions. Prefer simple words.
- Specific beats adjectives.
- Don't sell. Explain. Show.
- **Never use em dashes (—).** Use commas, periods, parentheses, semicolons, or colons.

## Forbidden words

revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge, seamless, empower, leverage,
unlock, transform, elevate, innovative, disruptive, ecosystem, robust,
enterprise-grade, next-generation, world-class, best-in-class,
state-of-the-art, industry-leading.

## Forbidden phrases

"We're redefining…", "Unlock your potential.", "The future of…",
"Powered by innovation.", "Built for modern businesses.",
"Scale effortlessly.", "Supercharge your workflow.",
"Experience seamless…", "Powerful yet simple."

## Examples

Good: Websites shouldn't need babysitting.
Bad: Revolutionizing publishing for the next generation.

Good: Most pages are cached automatically. You don't have to think about it.
Bad: Kumooo is incredibly fast.

Good: That login didn't work. Double-check your email and password.
Bad: Authentication failed.

Good: Nothing here yet. Let's fix that.
Bad: No content.

## Buttons

Prefer: Create site, Deploy, Save draft, Publish, View site, Delete, Restore.
Avoid: Continue, Proceed, Execute, Submit, Complete.

## Final review

Would a real engineer say this?
Does this sound like AI?
Am I overselling?
Can I make this shorter?
Did I accidentally use an em dash?

It's **Kumooo**, not Kumo.
