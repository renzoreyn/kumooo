A website is a bunch of files your computer can turn into pages, sitting on a computer that stays online (a host). People type a URL. The host sends those pages back.

## The three pieces

1. **Your files**: text, images, code. In kumooo.js that starts as a Next.js project on your laptop.
2. **A host**: [Cloudflare Workers](https://workers.cloudflare.com) on your account, or **kumooo hosting** at `{slug}.kumooo.site`.
3. **A URL**: something like `my-site.your-subdomain.workers.dev`, or `my-site.kumooo.site` on our Free plan. Later you can point your own domain at it (Pro+).

## What kumooo.js is in that picture

kumooo.js is not a mysterious new internet. It is starters, UI, and conventions on top of Next.js so you are not starting from a blank abyss.

- **Self-host:** you build and deploy on your own Cloudflare account.
- **Hosted:** Free gives you two sites and 150 MB of media on `kumooo.site`. See [Pricing](https://kumooo.dev/pricing).
