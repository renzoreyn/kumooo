# Blossom without the kitsch

May 2

Spring themes walk a line. Too pink and you look like a cafe menu. Too gray and why call it spring.

Haru's accent is cherry-blossom adjacent. Not literal petals. No emoji. Just a warm hint on links and focus rings so the page feels alive.

Color is context. I don't want your post about database migrations to look like a wedding invite. I want it to look like something you'd keep open in a tab while the kettle boils.

Season themes share one contract: home, post, page, archive, not-found. Haru interprets that contract softly. Wider margins on mobile. A measure that respects long sentences.

I test themes with boring content on purpose. If migrations and cache keys look good, your travel essay will look great. If only travel essays look good, the theme is lying.

Ship the theme, then forget it. That's the compliment I want. Your readers should remember the paragraph, not the border radius.
