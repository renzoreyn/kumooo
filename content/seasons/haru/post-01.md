# First warm day on the balcony

March 12

The radiator finally stopped fighting me. I opened the window and the air didn't feel like punishment.

I've been editing the haru stylesheet for two weeks. Mostly spacing. Mostly `--line` opacity. It sounds trivial until you read a long post and notice your eyes relax. That's the whole job for a reading theme: don't get in the way.

Yesterday I shipped a cache bump fix. Three lines. The kind of change that never makes a changelog but saves someone a confused refresh. I like those fixes. They're honest.

Today I'm writing this post from the balcony with bad wifi and good coffee. The draft autosaved twice while I stared at a pigeon. Fine. Spring is allowed to be slow.

If you're picking a theme for a personal site, ask one question: do you want the layout to feel like a desk or a billboard? Haru is the desk. Keep your titles short. Let the dates show. Publish when it's ready, not when it's loud.
