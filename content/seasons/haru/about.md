# About Haru

Pick **haru** when you want a site that reads like a quiet notebook, not a product launch. Soft type, calm spacing, and a home list that feels dated and personal. Good for essays, dev logs, field notes, and anything you'd write on a slow morning.

It defaults to light paper tones with a sakura-adjacent accent. Dark mode stays gentle. You don't need a hero banner or loud cards. The layout does the work.

Try it on [dash.kumooo.dev](https://dash.kumooo.dev) or read more at [kumooo.dev](https://kumooo.dev).

---

I'm Ren. I build Kumooo because I got tired of babysitting WordPress installs for sites that only needed markdown and a domain.

Haru is the season theme I reach for first. Spring energy without the clutter. The home page lists posts like entries in a journal: title, date, a line or two. Click through and you get a comfortable reading column. Nothing shouts.

This demo site is seeded content. The posts are fake observations about building, shipping, and noticing small things. They exist so you can see how haru handles real paragraphs, links, and lists before you paste your own draft.

If you're comparing season themes: haru is the soft default. Natsu is louder. Aki is editorial. Fuyu is minimal and cold. Start here if you're not sure.
