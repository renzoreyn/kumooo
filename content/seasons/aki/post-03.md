# Reflections after the first deploy

The first deploy is never pretty. You forget an env var. You typo a route. You discover that production DNS and your local hosts file disagree about what "ready" means. I still remember the relief when the 200 came back from a URL I didn't have to explain with "well, on my machine."

Writing about that moment months later is different from tweeting it live. Twitter rewards heat. An essay rewards structure. Aki assumes you might want both paragraphs in the same piece: the frantic play-by-play and the calmer postmortem. Chapter borders help mark that shift without forcing you into two posts.

I don't think every company needs a blog. I do think every builder benefits from a place that's slower than Slack and more durable than a gist. Markdown on Kumooo is that place for me. Draft, revise, publish. Revisions stay in the database even when the public URL looks static.

Choose this theme if your readers print posts or save them to read later. Choose it if you quote yourself in talks and want a canonical link. Choose something else if your content is mostly one-liners. Seasons are personalities, not ranks. Aki just happens to like sentences that finish their thought.
