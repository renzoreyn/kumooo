# Accent color is a contract

Pick one accent. Use it consistently. Stop.

Natsu runs a coastal-warm accent in light mode and a sharper coral in dark. Both pass contrast checks. Both show up on links, buttons, and card borders so interactive elements don't hide.

I watch founders gray everything out for "professionalism" and wonder why nobody clicks anything. Color isn't noise when it signals action.

Your brand might differ later. Theme Studio is coming. For now, seasons give you four intentional palettes so you don't start from zero.

Test with one real screenshot before you commit. Post it to your team chat. If nobody can find the link, the accent failed.

Also test focus rings with keyboard only. Summer themes get shared in sunny rooms where mouse precision is worse than you think. Visible focus is part of the accent contract too.

Screenshot your home page next to haru with the same four titles. Natsu should feel wider and louder without changing your words. That's the comparison worth doing before you commit.
