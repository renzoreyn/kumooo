# Cards beat walls of text on mobile

Try reading a 2014 blog theme on a phone. Tiny type. Edge-to-edge gray. You pinch-zoom like it's a PDF.

Natsu home uses cards so each post gets its own box. Your thumb knows where to tap. Your eye gets a boundary.

That's not decoration. It's navigation.

I see teams spend weeks on a hero animation and zero hours on list readability. Flip that. Most visits land on the index or an archive. Make those pages fast and obvious.

Write titles like push notifications without the guilt. Clear. Specific. No mystery meat clickbait.

Spacing between cards matters as much as spacing inside them. Summer heat makes dense UI feel worse. Give each entry air. Excerpt optional. Date visible. Link obvious.

Then publish and go outside. It's summer. The cache will keep up. Your readers on slow LTE will still get HTML in milliseconds.
